Four Datasets, One Story

These datasets document different moments in a long history of Indian land dispossession:

BLM Allotment Patents (1854–1960s) — The record of action. 285,870 General Land Office patents documenting every trust patent, fee patent, and conversion — the administrative machinery through which Indian land was allotted, held in trust, and removed from trust protection.

Wilson Report (1934) — The baseline. How much land had been allotted, and how much had already been sold off by the time the Indian Reorganization Act ended general allotment. Documents the cumulative damage of the allotment era across 212 reservations.

Murray Memorandum (1947–1957) — The second wave. Trust land removed during the termination era, when Congress pursued ending federal trust responsibilities. 18,546 trust removal transactions across 52 BIA agencies in a single decade.

Federal Register (1983) — The claims for redress. Nearly 50 years after the IRA, allottees and heirs filed formal claims asserting their land had been taken without consent. A fraction of all conversions, but the most detailed record of individual forced fee cases.
