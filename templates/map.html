How to Use This Map
Start with all patents loaded to see the national picture — 239,845 allotment patents across every reservation. Pan and zoom to explore regions. At wider zoom levels, patents appear as dots (centroids) for performance. Zoom in past a reservation level and they become parcel shapes showing actual land boundaries.

Use the tribe, state, and year filters on the left to narrow your view, then click Run Analysis to load detailed statistics and charts below.
Data Coverage Note
The research database contains 285,870 patents. This map displays 239,845 — the patents that could be matched to parcels in the Public Land Survey System (PLSS). Approximately 46,000 patents are not shown because they use non-rectangular survey descriptions that cannot be placed on the PLSS grid. The largest gaps are in Arizona and New Mexico, where Navajo, Gila River, and Colorado River reservation allotments used metes-and-bounds surveys.
